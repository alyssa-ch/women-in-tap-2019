Women in Tap

By Alyssa Choi

About

If you are interested in dance, feminism, or film, keep scrolling down! This website was created with the intention to educate readers about female tap dancers, particularly in the Classic Hollywood Film Era. The Classic Hollywood Film Era took place from the late 1920's to the early 1960's. Hope you enjoy this and find this educational!

Biographies

Read down below to learn about some of the many talented and incredible women tappers!

Anne Miller

Ann Miller was a child dance prodigy and was in many films such as Easter Parade, On the Town, Kiss Me Kate, and Stage Door. Miller was different from most tap dancers in that she was very tall, yet she could still tap just as well as, if not better than, others.

Fun facts:

  • Ann Miller was actually part Cherokee!
  • Her favorite pair of tap shoes, which she named "Moe and Joe," are exhibited in the Smithsonian Institution.

Ginger Rogers

Ginger Rogers was a major movie star in Hollywood's Golden Age, and she made 73 movies over the duration of her career, including Stage Door (with Ann Miller), Top Hat, Swing Time, and Kitty Foyle, for which she won the Academy Award for Best Actress. Rogers often starred alongside Fred Astaire, and they were revolutionary in that their dances were different, elegant, and virtuosic.

Fun facts:

  • Rogers' real name was Virginia Katherine Rogers!
  • Rogers is described as doing everything Astaire did, but "backwards and in high heels"!

Eleanor Powell

Eleanor Powell began studying tap at 16 years old and began to appear in Broadway shows. After making her Hollywood debut, she was later was one of MGM's top dancers during the Hollywood Golden Era. She starred in movies specifically tailored for her skills, such as Born to Dance, Broadway Melody of 1938, and Broadway Melody of 1940. Powell was even named the World's Greatest Tap Dancer by the Dance Masters of America!

Fun facts:

  • In the making of Broadway Melody of 1940, Fred Astaire was intimidated by Powell's amazing talent!
  • She was featured as a dancer in her first Hollywood film, George White's Scandals.

Rita Hayworth

Rita Hayworth was also a major star in Hollywood's Golden Era, appearing in 61 movies over 37 years. She starred in Cover Girl with Gene Kelly and You'll Never Get Rich with Fred Astaire. Astaire even called Hayworth his favorite dancing partner!

Fun facts:

  • Rita Hayworth's real name was Margarita Carmen Cansino!
  • She was referred to as "The Love Goddess" after becoming one of the most glamorous film stars of the 1940's.

Vera Ellen

Vera Ellen started dancing at 10 years old, and soon after joined the Rockettes. She first started her career in Broadway, appearing in several musicals. She later was in many movies, including On the Town (with Ann Miller, Gene Kelly, and Frank Sinatra) and White Christmas (with Bing Crosby and Danny Kaye - she also danced with John Brascia). While certainly not as famous as other stars, Ellen is known for her vivacity, vibrancy, and "elfin charm".

Fun facts:

  • Vera Ellen was named Vera-Ellen Westmeier Rohe (her actual name) because her mother had a dream about having a daughter named Vera-Ellen.
  • She was one of the youngest Rockettes!
